MSE Playground: Bias & Variance Trade-Off

Connect the pieces: Bias (systematic error) and Variance (wiggliness across samples). Their sum gives MSE.

Formula

MSE(θ̂) = Bias(θ̂)² + Var(θ̂)

  • Bias: how far the average prediction is from the truth.
  • Variance: how much predictions change if we re-sample the data.

Example Walkthrough

Lower bias isn’t always better if the variance shoots up. We care about the sum.
